Site Suitability analysis for hazardous site in Puerto Rico

Purpose: Site suitability analysis for the federal government of Puerto Rico to locate a hazardous material facility with the following criteria:
- The sites must be only in these three hydrological zones:Interior Puerto Rico, Cibuco Guajataca or Culebrinas-Guanajibo.
- Must be within 2 miles of roads that has a road type (RDLNTYPE) of 2, OR must be within 5 miles of railroads.
- Must NOT be within 5 miles of airports.
- Must NOT be within 10 miles of dams.

Inspiration

This was part of a personal research project to explore the different ways of using data and also the fact that the people in Puerto Rico dispose of about 5.56 pounds of waste per person per day which is more than the national average in the United States of America.

Process

The vector data of airports, hydro-regions, railroads and roads were added to Arcgis Pro. The 3 hydro-zones of interests were selected by location and the data were saved in a shapefile. The same process was carried out for dams and airports within the hydro zones of interests. Roads and rail data in the areas of interests were then merged. 2 miles buffer zones were created around the merged roads and rail. 5 miles buffer zones were then created around the airports. 10 miles buffer were also created around the dams. Unions created around the airport buffer and dams buffer using the union analysis tools. Erase tools were used to erase the intersection. It combined the two layers and kept the areas that are not overlapping. These areas are considered the hazardous areas which are not suitable for setting up the areas. Note:Remember also that the areas should also be within the three select hydrological zones. A clip tool was therefore used to keep the areas which are within the 3 hydrological zones (By clipping the hazardous sites and the areas of interests i.e the 3 hydrological zones) Using the measure distance tool to verify the distances and if we have met all the criteria. The map details such as legends, key, scale and headings were then added. Tools and Technologies used:
Arcgis Pro, merge tools, buffer tool, clip tool, union tool,clip tool, erase tool.
